🍳 Masala Scrambled Eggs with Whole Wheat Roti
Ingredients
Whole Eggs4 eggs (~240g)
Egg Whites2 whites (~60g)
Whole Wheat Roti2 rotis (~60g dough)
Olive Oil1 tsp (5ml)
Onion (chopped)2 tbsp (~30g)
Green Chilli1 small
Turmeric¼ tsp
Saltto taste
Coriander leavessmall handful
Steps
  1. 1Heat olive oil in a non-stick pan over medium heat. Add chopped onion and green chilli; sauté for 1–2 mins until soft.
  2. 2Whisk all 4 eggs + 2 egg whites together in a bowl with turmeric and a pinch of salt.
  3. 3Pour egg mixture into the pan. Stir gently with a spatula on low heat — don't overcook. Remove when just set.
  4. 4Garnish with fresh coriander. Serve with 2 freshly made whole wheat rotis.

🐟 150g Rohu Fish grilled/curry

🥗 Mixed Salad

🐟 Bengali Light Rohu Curry + Brown Rice
Ingredients
Rohu Fish fillet150g
Brown Rice (dry)75g (~1.5 cups cooked)
Mustard Oil1 tsp (5ml)
Onion (medium)½ (~60g)
Tomato1 medium (~100g)
Ginger-Garlic Paste1 tsp
Turmeric½ tsp
Cumin Seeds¼ tsp
Red Chilli Powder½ tsp
Saltto taste
Cucumber½ (~80g) for salad
Onion (for salad)¼ (~30g)
Steps
  1. 1Wash brown rice and cook with 2x water (150ml water for 75g rice) in a pot for 35–40 mins or pressure cooker for 3 whistles.
  2. 2Marinate fish in ¼ tsp turmeric + pinch of salt for 5 mins.
  3. 3Heat mustard oil in a pan. Lightly pan-fry the fish for 2–3 mins per side on medium heat. Remove and set aside.
  4. 4In the same pan, add cumin seeds. Once they splutter, add onion and sauté until golden (~3 mins).
  5. 5Add ginger-garlic paste, remaining turmeric, red chilli, and salt. Cook for 1 min. Add tomato and cook until soft.
  6. 6Add ½ cup water, bring to simmer, then gently place fish back in. Cook 5 mins on low heat.
  7. 7Serve with rice and a simple salad of sliced cucumber + onion with lemon and salt.

🍗 150g Chicken Breast grilled/tandoori
🫘 1 cup Dal
🫓 2 Whole Wheat Rotis
🥦 Mixed Veggies
🍗 Tandoori-Style Chicken Breast + Masoor Dal
Ingredients — Chicken
Chicken Breast150g
Ginger-Garlic Paste1½ tsp
Lemon Juice1 tbsp (15ml)
Cumin Powder½ tsp
Coriander Powder½ tsp
Red Chilli Powder½ tsp
Turmeric¼ tsp
Salt¾ tsp
Ingredients — Dal
Masoor Dal (red lentils)50g dry (~½ cup)
Onion½ medium (~60g)
Tomato1 small (~80g)
Ginger-Garlic Paste1 tsp
Mustard Oil1 tsp
Turmeric + Salt½ tsp + to taste
Steps
  1. 1Marinate chicken breast in all spices + lemon juice for at least 30 mins (overnight = best flavour).
  2. 2Cook in an oven at 200°C for 20–22 mins, or on a grill pan over high heat for 5–6 mins per side.
  3. 3For dal: boil masoor dal with 200ml water and ¼ tsp turmeric for 15–20 mins until soft.
  4. 4Prepare tadka: heat oil, add onion until golden, add ginger-garlic paste, tomato, and salt. Cook 3 mins.
  5. 5Pour tadka into cooked dal, stir and simmer for 5 mins.
  6. 6Serve chicken with dal, 2 freshly made whole wheat rotis, and steamed/stir-fried veggies (broccoli, carrot, beans).

ESSENTIAL TIPS
For fat loss, muscle gain, and staying consistent
💧
Hydration
Drink 3–4 litres of water daily. Carry a 1L bottle and refill it 3–4 times. Dehydration kills performance and hunger cues.
😴
Sleep
7–8 hours of sleep every night. Growth hormone peaks during deep sleep — this is when your muscles actually grow.
📈
Progressive Overload
Add 2.5kg or 1 extra rep every 1–2 weeks. Without progressive overload, your muscles have no reason to grow.
⏱️
Meal Timing
Try to eat within 60 mins of waking up. Space meals every 3–4 hours. Don't skip the post-workout meal window.
🚶
Daily Steps
Aim for 8,000–10,000 steps/day. Even on rest days, a 30-min brisk walk boosts calorie burn and mood.
🍷
Alcohol
Limit alcohol — it blocks protein synthesis by up to 30%, adds empty calories, and disrupts deep sleep.
🎯
Consistency
Results come in months, not weeks. Missing 1 meal or workout won't hurt you — quitting will. Stay the course.
📸
Track Progress
Take photos every 2 weeks in same lighting + pose. Scale weight fluctuates daily — photos don't lie.
🧂
Cooking for Fat Loss
Grill, boil, or air-fry your proteins. Avoid deep frying. Use mustard/olive oil in small amounts.
