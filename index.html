🐟 Bengali Light Rohu Curry + Brown Rice
Ingredients
Rohu Fish fillet150g
Brown Rice (dry)75g (~1.5 cups cooked)

Turmeric½ tsp
Cumin Seeds¼ tsp
Red Chilli Powder½ tsp
Saltto taste
Cucumber½ (~80g) for salad
Onion (for salad)¼ (~30g)
Steps
  1. 1Wash brown rice and cook with 2x water (150ml water for 75g rice) in a pot for 35–40 mins or pressure cooker for 3 whistles.

Arm Circles + Wrist Rotations
30 sec each direction
Shoulders
✓
How to do it
1Extend both arms to the sides at shoulder height.
2Make small circles forward for 15 sec, then large circles for 15 sec.
3Reverse direction. Then rotate wrists forward and backward 10× each.
Tips
Keep shoulders down — don't shrug them up toward ears

Main Workout
01
Standard Push-Ups
4 × 15–20 reps · 60s rest
Chest
✓
Setup
1Place hands slightly wider than shoulder-width, fingers pointing forward.
2Arms fully extended, body in a straight line from head to heels.
3Brace core, squeeze glutes — your body should be rigid like a plank.
Movement
4Lower chest to floor over 2–3 seconds — elbows at ~45° to body (not flared out wide).
5Push back up explosively. Squeeze chest at the top.
Key Tips
Chest should touch or nearly touch the floor — half reps don't count
Keep neck neutral — don't drop head or crane it forward
Slow descent = more muscle activation. 3 sec down, 1 sec up
Common Mistakes
Hips sagging or raised — body must be one straight line
Elbows flaring out 90° — causes shoulder impingement
Half reps — go all the way down every time
02
Wide-Grip Push-Ups
3 × 12–15 reps · 60s rest
Outer Chest
✓
Setup
1Place hands 1.5× shoulder-width apart, slightly wider than standard.
2Fingers angled slightly outward (about 15–20°).
Movement
3Lower chest between hands — elbows track outward more than standard push-up.
4Press up and feel the outer pecs squeeze at the top.
Key Tips
Focus on feeling the stretch across your chest at the bottom
Don't lock elbows at the top — keep tension on chest
Common Mistakes
Hands too wide — strains wrists and shoulders
Core not braced — lower back arches
03
Incline Push-Ups (hands on chair)
3 × 15 reps · 60s rest
Lower Chest
✓
Setup
1Place both hands on the edge of a sturdy chair or bed. Arms straight, body at ~45° angle.
2Body must be in a straight line from head to heels — no hip drop.
Movement
3Bend elbows and lower chest toward the chair edge.
4Push back up fully. The higher the surface, the easier — use a lower surface to progress.
Key Tips
Targets the lower chest — really squeeze at the top of each rep
Make sure chair is against a wall so it doesn't slide
04
Decline Push-Ups (feet on chair)
3 × 10–12 reps · 90s rest
Upper Chest
✓
Setup
1Place feet on chair or bed, hands on the floor at shoulder width.
2Body forms a downward angle — hips, back, and neck all aligned.
Movement
3Lower nose toward floor slowly, elbows at ~45° to body.
4Push back up strongly. This hits the upper chest and front shoulders hard.
Key Tips
The higher your feet, the harder and more upper-chest focused it gets
This is the hardest push-up variation — take extra rest if needed
Common Mistakes
Hips rising up — keep them in line with your torso
Looking straight down — look slightly ahead to keep neck neutral
05
Diamond Push-Ups
3 × 10–12 reps · 60s rest
Triceps
✓
Setup
1Place hands under your chest, touching thumbs and index fingers together to form a diamond shape.
2Arms extended, body in a straight plank line.
Movement
3Lower chest toward your hands — elbows will naturally point backward and stay close to body.
4Push up powerfully. Feel the back of your arms (triceps) doing the work.
Key Tips
Keep elbows tucked — do NOT let them flare out to the sides
If wrists hurt, make fists instead of flat palms
This is very tricep-focused — expect fatigue quickly
Common Mistakes
Elbows flaring wide — removes tricep emphasis, strains shoulders
Hands too far from chest — shifts load to chest, less tricep activation
06
Chair Dips
3 × 12–15 reps · 60s rest
Triceps
✓
Setup
1Sit on the edge of a sturdy chair. Place hands on the edge beside your hips, fingers forward.
2Slide your hips off the chair. Keep feet flat or legs extended (harder).
Movement
3Bend elbows straight back and lower your body until upper arms are parallel to floor (90°).
4Press back up through the palms. Don't use legs to help.
Key Tips
Keep hips close to the chair — don't drift forward
Keep chest up and shoulders back and down throughout
The closer your feet, the easier. Legs extended = harder
Common Mistakes
Elbows flaring out to sides — must point straight back
Going too deep — stop at 90° to protect shoulder joint
Shoulders shrugging up — keep them packed down
07
Pike Push-Ups
3 × 10–12 reps · 60s rest
Shoulders
✓
Setup
1Start in a push-up position, then walk feet toward hands to form an inverted V shape (like a downward dog).
2Hips high in the air, head between arms, back straight.
Movement
3Bend elbows and lower the top of your head toward the floor.
4Press back up through shoulders. This mimics a shoulder press movement.
Key Tips
The more vertical your body, the more it targets shoulders over chest
Walk feet closer to hands for more shoulder activation
Look at the floor between your hands — don't crank your neck
Common Mistakes
Hips dropping — keep them high throughout the movement
Barely bending elbows — go until head nearly touches floor

Keep core engaged — don't arch lower back

01
Pull-Ups or Negative Pull-Ups
4 × max reps · 2 min rest
Back / Biceps
✓
Pull-Up Setup
1Hang from a bar with overhand grip, hands slightly wider than shoulders.
2Dead hang — arms fully extended, shoulders engaged (not shrugged up).
Movement
3Drive elbows down toward your hips — think "elbow to back pocket".
4Pull until chin clears the bar. Lower under control over 3 seconds.
Negative Pull-Up (if you can't do full pull-ups)
5Use a chair to jump to the top position (chin above bar).
6Remove feet, lower yourself as slowly as possible (aim for 5–8 seconds).
7Do 5×5 negatives — in 4–6 weeks you'll do full pull-ups.
Key Tips
Don't kip or swing — strict reps only for muscle growth
Retract shoulder blades before pulling — protects the joint
Full range: dead hang at bottom, chin over bar at top
Common Mistakes
Shrugging shoulders up — pack them down before you pull
Half reps — not reaching chin over bar
Swinging body for momentum — it defeats the purpose
02
Door Frame Rows (towel)
4 × 12–15 reps · 90s rest
Mid-Back
✓
Setup
1Loop a folded towel around a door handle. Hold both ends with both hands.

Movement

Keep elbows close to body and pointing backward

Body not straight — hips must not drop or pike up

Breathe out as you lift, breathe in as you lower

Bouncing up and down — slow and controlled only

3 × 20–30 sec hold
Shoulders / Core
✓
How to kick up
1Face away from wall. Place hands 15–20cm from wall, shoulder-width apart.
2Kick one leg up, then the other, to get heels resting against the wall.
3Hold: arms locked, core tight, glutes squeezed, body as vertical as possible.

03
Lateral Raise (water bottles)
3 × 15 reps · 60s rest
Lateral Delt
✓
Setup
1Hold a full 1L water bottle in each hand. Stand tall, slight bend in knees.
2Arms at sides, slight bend in elbows (not locked straight).
Movement
3Raise both arms out to sides simultaneously — stop at shoulder height (no higher).
4Lower slowly over 3 seconds. The lowering builds as much muscle as the lift.
Key Tips
Lead with your elbows, not your hands — it activates lateral delt better
Tilt the bottle slightly so pinky side is higher (like pouring water) — hits delt harder
Don't shrug — shoulders stay packed down throughout
Common Mistakes
Swinging body to get bottles up — use lighter bottles, strict form only
Going above shoulder height — strains AC joint
04
Plank
4 × 45–60 sec holds
Core
✓
Setup
1Forearms on floor, elbows under shoulders. Body forms a straight line — head to heels.
2Brace abs hard (as if about to take a punch), squeeze glutes, press forearms into floor.
Key Tips
Breathe steadily — don't hold your breath or the time will feel impossible
Push forearms into floor to engage shoulders and lats too
Quality: a 30-sec perfect plank beats a 60-sec sagging one
Common Mistakes
Hips too high — lowers core engagement
Hips sagging — lower back strain, stop and reset
Head dropping — keep neck neutral, eyes looking down
05
Bicycle Crunches
3 × 20 reps each side
Abs
✓
How to do it
1Lie on back, hands lightly behind head (don't pull neck), knees at 90°.
2Lift shoulders off floor. Bring right knee toward chest while rotating left elbow to meet it.
3Extend right leg out, switch to left knee + right elbow. That's 1 rep each side.
Key Tips
Rotate your torso — not just your elbow. Think shoulder to knee
Go SLOW — fast bicycle crunches activate momentum, not abs
Keep lower back pressed into floor throughout
Common Mistakes
Pulling neck with hands — causes strain, hands just support lightly
Going too fast — bicycles done correctly should feel hard
06
Leg Raises (floor)
3 × 15 reps
Lower Abs
✓
How to do it
1Lie flat on back, hands under your hips or at sides, legs straight.
2Keeping legs straight, raise them to 90° (pointing at ceiling).

Dropping legs too fast — controlled lowering is the whole point

ESSENTIAL TIPS
For fat loss, muscle gain, and staying consistent
💧
Hydration
Drink 3–4 litres of water daily. Carry a 1L bottle and refill it 3–4 times. Dehydration kills performance and hunger cues.
😴
Sleep
7–8 hours of sleep every night. Growth hormone peaks during deep sleep — this is when your muscles actually grow.
📈
Progressive Overload
Add 2.5kg or 1 extra rep every 1–2 weeks. Without progressive overload, your muscles have no reason to grow.
⏱️
Meal Timing
Try to eat within 60 mins of waking up. Space meals every 3–4 hours. Don't skip the post-workout meal window.
🚶
Daily Steps
Aim for 8,000–10,000 steps/day. Even on rest days, a 30-min brisk walk boosts calorie burn and mood.
🍷
Alcohol
Limit alcohol — it blocks protein synthesis by up to 30%, adds empty calories, and disrupts deep sleep.
🎯
Consistency
Results come in months, not weeks. Missing 1 meal or workout won't hurt you — quitting will. Stay the course.
📸
Track Progress
Take photos every 2 weeks in same lighting + pose. Scale weight fluctuates daily — photos don't lie.
🧂
Cooking for Fat Loss
Grill, boil, or air-fry your proteins. Avoid deep frying. Use mustard/olive oil in small amounts.
